Transformer DC Website Re-design

Transformer, a 501(c)(3) non-profit based in Washington, DC, is dedicated to supporting emerging artists and arts leaders through innovative exhibitions and programs. Founded in June 2002, its mission emphasizes inclusivity, professional excellence, and the freedom of artistic expression. In our quest to enhance digital accessibility and engagement, we're redesigning our website to reflect these core values. This initiative will make our diverse, contemporary art more accessible, ensuring our online platform is as inclusive and engaging as our physical exhibitions and programs.

Research & Ideation

Current Site

Upon reviewing the Transformer website, our team identified a critical need for a redesign. The site's design elements were overly bold and the navigation proved to be complex, hindering easy access. Our objective is to create a more streamlined and visually appealing interface, enhancing overall user experience and accessibility.

Surveys and Interviews

We conducted surveys and interviews to hone in on what could be improved further on. During User Interviews, we discovered that our NGO's website is hard to navigate. Therefore, we believe that improving the design, accessibility features, and interactivity of the website will benefit the organization. We might do this by having clearer CTA, and redesigning the navigation bar. Doing this will allow our product not only have a cleaner, more sleek look but bring more users to the site.

User Persona

Based on the comprehensive insights from our interviews, we crafted an official user persona that encapsulates the aspirations, needs, and challenges faced by emerging artists like Alexa, guiding our website redesign to better serve and engage our artistic community.

Problem Statement

The current website for our art exhibit faces accessibility issues, hindering its usability for a diverse and general audience. This lack of accessibility creates barriers for individuals with disabilities and limits the overall inclusivity of the exhibition. To address this challenge, there is a pressing need to redesign the online exhibit platform, ensuring it complies with accessibility standards and provides a seamless and enjoyable experience for all users. This transformation will not only align with the principles of inclusivity but also expand the reach of the art exhibit to a broader audience, fostering a more diverse and engaged community of art enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Having finalized our high-fidelity prototypes, we are ready to embark on an exciting phase of development that promises to redefine the NGO's presence and the DC art community's engagement. Our vision extends beyond our current timeline; with more time, we would have integrated virtual artist gallery spaces into our platform. These virtual galleries, designed to enhance user interaction and broaden our reach, would feature immersive 3D gallery tours, dynamic art installations, artist-curated exhibitions, and live artistic events.
